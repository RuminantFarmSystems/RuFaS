# Feed Storage Theoretical Documentation

This document details how stored feeds lose dry matter and nutrients.

## 1 Dry Matter Loss

### 1.1 Grain

The two types of grain simulated in RuFaS are dry and high-moisture.

The cumulative amount of dry matter lost from dry grain is calculated with the equation:

$$
L_{gaseous} = M_i \cdot 0.01 \tag{1.1.1}
$$

Where $L_{gaseous}$ is the total amount of dry matter lost as gas (kg) and $M_i$ is the mass of dry matter at the time of storage (kg).

The cumulative amount of dry matter lost from high-moisture grain is calculated with the equation:

$$
L_{gaseous} = M_i \cdot 0.05 \tag{1.1.2}
$$

Where ${L}_{gaseous}$ is the total amount of dry matter lost as gas (kg) and $M_i$ is the mass of dry matter at the time of storage (kg).

### 1.2 Hay

Four types of hay are simulated by RuFaS: protected-indoor, protected-wrapped, protected-tarped, and unprotected-outdoor.
The cumulative amount of gaseous dry matter loss that has happened since hay storage is calculated with the equation:

$$
L_{gaseous} = L_{base} + L_{additional} \tag{1.2.1}
$$

Where $L_{base}$ is the total amount of gaseous dry matter loss from hay after storage (kg) and $L_{additional}$ is the total amount of additional dry matter loss from hay due to weather and storage conditions (kg).

$L_{base}$ is calculated with the equation:

$$
L_{base} = L_{first 30 days} + L_{post 30 days} \tag{1.2.2}
$$

Where $L_{first 30 days}$ is the total amount of gaseous dry matter loss that occurred in the first 30 days of storage (kg), and $L_{post 30 days}$ is the total amount of gaseous dry matter loss that occurred after the first 30 days of storage (kg).

$L_{first 30 days}$ is calculated with the equation:

$$
L_{first 30 days} = Q + 2433(\frac{moisture - MF(1 - moisture)(\frac{1}{1 - 0.004d})}{(1-moisture)(14206-2433[0.004d])(\frac{1}{1-0.004d})}) \tag{1.2.3}
$$

Where $L_{first 30 days}$ is the total gaseous dry matter loss that occurred in the hay in the first 30 days of storage ($kg$), $Q$ is the total sensible heat generated by the hay ($kJ/kg$), $moisture$ is the fraction of fresh mass in the hay that is not dry matter when the hay is stored (unitless), $MF$ is the final moisture fraction of hay (unitless), and $d$ is the number of days the hay has been stored for.

The total sensible heat generated is generated with the equation:

$$
Q = 104 \cdot moisture^{2.18} \cdot {b_{density}}^{0.5} + 5.72(moisture^{1.23} \cdot {b_{density}}^{0.94}) \tag{1.2.4}
$$

Where $moisture$ is the fraction of fresh mass in the hay that is not dry matter when the hay is stored (unitless) and $b_{density}$ is the density of the hay bale when stored ($kg / m^3$).

$b_{density}$ is calculated with the equation:

$$
b_{density} = 100 + 440moisture \tag{1.2.5}
$$

Where $moisture$ is the fraction of fresh mass in the hay that is not dry matter when the hay is stored (unitless).

The final moisture fraction of hay is defined as

$$
MF = 0.12 \tag{1.2.6}
$$

$L_{post 30 days}$ is calculated with the equation:

$$
L_{post 30 days} = 0.0001 \cdot max(0, d - 30) \tag{1.2.7}
$$

Where $d$ is the number of days that the hay has been stored for.

$L_{additional}$ is calculated for protected wrapped, protected tarped and unprotected outdoor hay with the equation:

$$
L_{additional} = \sum_{i=1}^{d} l \cdot rain_i \cdot max(0.0, \frac{high_i + low_i}{2}) \cdot \frac{1}{b_{density}} \cdot {b_{size}}^3 \tag{1.2.8}
$$

Where $d$ is the number of days the hay has been stored for, $i$ is the day since storage that dry matter loss is being calculated for, $l$ is the loss coefficient (unitless), $rain_i$ is the amount of rain on day $i$ (cm), $high_i$ is the high temperature on day $i$ ($C^\circ$), $low_i$ is the low temperature on day $i$ ($C^\circ$), b_{density} is the bale density ($kg / m^3$) (eqn. 1.2.5), and $b_{size}$ is the diameter of the hay bale ($m$).

The loss coefficients for the different hay storages are defined in the table:
| Hay Storage Type  | Loss Coefficient |
|-------------------|------------------|
|Protected Wrapped  |0.0000216         |
|Protected Tarped   |0.0000108         |
|Unprotected Outdoor|0.00006           |

$L_{additional}$ for protected indoor hay is 0.

### 1.3 Silage and Baleage

Both silage and baleage experience dry matter loss to fermentation.

The cumulative dry matter loss to fermentation for ensiled alfalfa ($kg$) is calculated with the equation:

$$
L_{fermentation} = \sum_{i=1}^{d} drymass_{i-1} \cdot (0.0156 - 0.0364(dryfrac_{i-1} - 0.2)) \tag{1.3.1}
$$

Where $d$ is the number of days the alfalfa has been ensiled for, $i$ is the day since ensilement that dry matter loss is being calculated for, $drymass_{i-1}$ is the dry matter mass of ensiled alfalfa on day $i-1$, and $dryfrac_{i-1}$ is the fraction of ensiled alfalfa fresh mass that is dry matter on day $i-1$. When $i$ is 1, $drymass_{i-1}$ is the initial amount of ensiled alfalfa dry mass and $dryfrac_{i-1}$ is the initial dry matter fraction of the ensiled alfalfa. This equation is only appropriate for use if $dryfrac_{i-1}$ is in the range $[0.2, 0.6]$ and the average temperature on day $i$ is in the range $[5, 45]$ ($C^\circ$).

The cumulative dry matter loss to fermentation for ensiled corn, grass, and small grain ($kg$) is calculated with the equation:

$$
L_{fermentation} = \sum_{i=1}^{d} drymass_{i-1} \cdot (0.00864 - 0.0193(dryfrac_{i-1} - 0.15)) \tag{1.3.2}
$$

Where $d$ is the number of days the crop has been ensiled for, $i$ is the day since ensilement that dry matter loss is being calculated for, $drymass_{i-1}$ is the dry matter mass of ensiled crop on day $i-1$, and $dryfrac_{i-1}$ is the fraction of the ensiled crop fresh mass that is dry matter on day $i-1$. When $i$ is 1, $drymass_{i-1}$ is the initial amount of ensiled crop dry mass and $dryfrac_{i-1}$ is the initial dry matter fraction of the ensiled crop. This equation is only appropriate for use if $dryfrac_{i-1}$ is in the range $[0.15, 0.6]$ and the average temperature on day $i$ is in the range $[0, 40]$ ($C^\circ$).

#### 1.3.1 Effluent Loss

Ensiled crops lose both moisture and dry matter mass to effluent (but baled crops do not).

If a crop is ensiled with a dry matter fraction of less than 0.3, it will lose dry matter and moisture to effluent.

The cumulative amount of dry matter lost to effluent ($kg$) is calculated with the equation:

$$
DL_{effluent} = dryfrac_{effluent} \cdot M_{effluent} \cdot 0.1 \cdot max(10, d) \tag{1.3.1.1}
$$

Where $dryfrac_{effluent}$ is the fraction of dry matter in the effluent that is lost, $M_{effluent}$ is the estimated maximum effluent ($kg$), and $d$ is the number of days the crop has been ensiled for.

The cumulative amount of moisture lost to effluent ($kg$) is calculated with the equation:

$$
ML_{effluent} = (1 - dryfrac_{effluent}) \cdot M_{effluent} \cdot 0.1 \cdot max(10, d) \tag{1.3.1.2}
$$

Where $dryfrac_{effluent}$ is the fraction of dry matter in the effluent that is lost, $M_{effluent}$ is the estimated maximum effluent ($kg$), and $d$ is the number of days the crop has been ensiled for.

The fraction of dry matter in effluent is a constant:

$$
dryfrac_{effluent} = 0.1035 \tag{1.3.1.3}
$$

The estimated maximum effluent for an ensiled crop is calculated with the following equation:

$$
M_{effluent} = mass_{fresh} \cdot ((1 - dryfrac) - 0.7) \tag{1.3.1.4}
$$

Where $mass_{fresh}$ is the fresh mass of the crop when it is ensiled ($kg$) and $dryfrac$ is the fraction of the crop's fresh mass which is dry matter when it is ensiled.

## 2 Nutrient Loss

Nutrient loss associated with loss of dry matter from stored crops is calculated for hay, silage and baleage.

The equation for recalculating the percentage of dry matter nurient:

$$
n_{updated} = \frac{max(0, \frac{n_{initial}}{100} - C) \cdot DL_{fraction}}{1 - DL_{fraction}} \cdot 100 \tag{2.1}
$$

Where $n_{updated}$ is the percentage of the target nutrient in the stored crop's dry matter mass after accounting for dry matter loss, $n_{initial}$ is the percentage of the target nutrient in the stored crop's dry matter mass before accounting for dry matter loss, $C$ is the fractional loss coefficient specific to the nutrient and storage type, and $DL_{fraction}$ is the fraction of dry matter lost.

$DL_{fraction}$ is calculated with the equation:

$$
DL_{fraction} = \frac{DM_{updated}}{DM_{initial}} \tag{2.2}
$$

Where $DM_{updated}$ is the dry matter mass in the stored crop after accounting for dry matter loss ($kg$), and $DM_{initial}$ is the amount of dry matter mass in the stored crop before accounting for dry matter loss ($kg$).

#### 2.2.1 Nutrient Loss to Effluent

When recalculating nutrient percentages after the loss of dry matter to effluent, the fractional loss coefficients used in equation $2.1$ must be calculated based on initial amounts of nutrients and the amount of dry matter lost.

The fractional loss coefficient for crude protein (CP) is calculated with the equation:

$$
C_{CP} = \frac{CP_{initial} - 0.3 \cdot DL_{fraction}}{1 - DL_{fraction}} \tag{2.2.1.1}
$$

Where $CP_{initial}$ is the percentage of dry matter mass that is crude protein in the ensiled crop before accounting for dry matter loss to effluent and $DL_{fraction}$ is the fraction of dry matter mass lost to effluent.

The fractional loss coefficient for non-protein nitrogen (NPN) is calculated with the equation:

$$
C_{NPN} = \frac{NPN_{initial} \cdot CP_{initial} - 0.3 \cdot DL_{fraction}}{CP_{initial} - DL_{fraction}} \tag{2.2.1.2}
$$

Where $NPN_{initial}$ is percentage of dry matter mass that is non-protein nitrogen in the ensiled crop before accouting for dry matter loss to effluent, $CP_{initial}$ is the percentage of dry matter mass that is crude protein before accounting for dry matter loss to effluent, and $DL_{fraction}$ is the fraction of dry matter mass lost to effluent.

$DL_{fraction}$ is calculated with the equation:

$$
DL_{fraction} = \frac{DM_{updated}}{DM_{initial}} \tag{2.2.1.3}
$$

Where $DM_{updated}$ is the amount of dry matter mass in the ensiled crop after accounting for dry matter lost to effluent, and $DM_{initial}$ is the amount of dry matter mass in the ensiled crop before accounting for dry matter lost to effluent.

# Feed Storage Theoretical Documentation

This document details how stored feeds lose dry matter and nutrients.

## 1 Dry Matter Loss

### 1.1 Grain

The two types of grain simulated in RuFaS are dry and high-moisture.

The cumulative amount of dry matter lost from dry grain is calculated with the equation:

$$
L_{gaseous} = M_i * 0.01 \tag{1.1.1}
$$

Where $L_{gaseous}$ is the total amount of dry matter lost as gas (kg) and $M_i$ is the mass of dry matter at the time of storage (kg).

The cumulative amount of dry matter lost from high-moisture grain is calculated with the equation:

$$
L_{gaseous} = M_i * 0.05 \tag{1.1.2}
$$

Where ${L}_{gaseous}$ is the total amount of dry matter lost as gas (kg) and $M_i$ is the mass of dry matter at the time of storage (kg).

### 1.2 Hay

Four types of hay are simulated by RuFaS: protected-indoor, protected-wrapped, protected-tarped, and unprotected-outdoor.
The cumulative amount of gaseous dry matter loss that has happened since hay storage is calculated with the equation:

$$
L_{gaseous} = L_{base} + L_{additional} \tag{1.2.1}
$$

Where $L_{base}$ is the total amount of gaseous dry matter loss from hay after storage (kg) and $L_{additional}$ is the total amount of additional dry matter loss from hay due to weather and storage conditions (kg).

$L_{base}$ is calculated with the equation:

$$
L_{base} = L_{first 30 days} + L_{post 30 days} \tag{1.2.2}
$$

Where $L_{first 30 days}$ is the total amount of gaseous dry matter loss that occurred in the first 30 days of storage (kg), and $L_{post 30 days}$ is the total amount of gaseous dry matter loss that occurred after the first 30 days of storage (kg).

$L_{first 30 days}$ is calculated with the equation:

$$
L_{first 30 days} = Q + 2433(\frac{moisture - MF(1 - moisture)(\frac{1}{1 - 0.004d})}{(1-moisture)(14206-2433[0.004d])(\frac{1}{1-0.004d})}) \tag{1.2.3}
$$

Where $L_{first 30 days}$ is the total gaseous dry matter loss that occurred in the hay in the first 30 days of storage ($kg$), $Q$ is the total sensible heat generated by the hay ($kJ/kg$), $moisture$ is the fraction of fresh mass in the hay that is not dry matter when the hay is stored (unitless), $MF$ is the final moisture fraction of hay (unitless), and $d$ is the number of days the hay has been stored for.

The total sensible heat generated is generated with the equation:

$$
Q = 104 * moisture^{2.18} * {b_{density}}^{0.5} + 5.72(moisture^{1.23} * {b_{density}}^{0.94}) \tag{1.2.4}
$$

Where $moisture$ is the fraction of fresh mass in the hay that is not dry matter when the hay is stored (unitless) and $b_{density}$ is the density of the hay bale when stored ($kg / m^3$).

$b_{density}$ is calculated with the equation:

$$
b_{density} = 100 + 440moisture \tag{1.2.5}
$$

Where $moisture$ is the fraction of fresh mass in the hay that is not dry matter when the hay is stored (unitless).

The final moisture fraction of hay is defined as

$$
MF = 0.12 \tag{1.2.6}
$$

$L_{post 30 days}$ is calculated with the equation:

$$
L_{post 30 days} = 0.0001 * max(0, d - 30) \tag{1.2.7}
$$

Where $d$ is the number of days that the hay has been stored for.

$L_{additional}$ is calculated for protected wrapped, protected tarped and unprotected outdoor hay with the equation:

$$
L_{additional} = \sum_{i=1}^{d} l \cdot rain_i \cdot max(0.0, \frac{high_i + low_i}{2}) \cdot \frac{1}{b_{density}} \cdot {b_{size}}^3 \tag{1.2.8}
$$

Where $d$ is the number of days the hay has been stored for, $i$ is the day since storage that dry matter loss is being calculated for, $l$ is the loss coefficient (unitless), $rain_i$ is the amount of rain on day $i$ (cm), $high_i$ is the high temperature on day $i$ ($C^\circ$), $low_i$ is the low temperature on day $i$ ($C^\circ$), b_{density} is the bale density ($kg / m^3$) (eqn. 1.2.5), and $b_{size}$ is the diameter of the hay bale ($m$).

### 1.3 Silage and Baleage

Both silage and baleage experience dry matter loss to fermentation.

The cumulative dry matter loss to fermentation for ensiled alfalfa ($kg$) is calculated with the equation:

$$
L_{fermentation} = \sum_{i=1}^{d} 0.0156 - 0.0364(dryfrac_{i-1} - 0.2) \tag{1.3.1}
$$

Where $d$ is the number of days the alfalfa has been ensiled for, $i$ is the day since ensilement that dry matter loss is being calculated for, and $dryfrac_{i-1}$ is the fraction of ensiled alfalfa fresh mass that is dry matter on day $i-1$. When $i$ is 1, $dryfrac_{i-1}$ is the fraction of ensiled alfalfa's fresh mass that was dry matter when the crop was initially ensiled. This equation is only appropriate for use if $dryfrac_{i-1}$ is in the range $[0.2, 0.6]$ and the average temperature on day $i$ is in the range $[5, 45]$ ($C^\circ$).

The cumulative dry matter loss to fermentation for ensiled corn, grass, and small grain ($kg$) is calculated with the equation:

$$
L_{fermentation} = \sum_{i=1}^{d} 0.00864 - 0.0193(dryfrac_{i-1} - 0.15) \tag{1.3.1}
$$

Where $d$ is the number of days the crop has been ensiled for, $i$ is the day since ensilement that dry matter loss is being calculated for, and $dryfrac_{i-1}$ is the fraction of the ensiled crop fresh mass that is dry matter on day $i-1$. When $i$ is 1, $dryfrac_{i-1}$ is the fraction of the ensiled crop's fresh mass that was dry matter when the crop was ensiled. This equation is only appropriate for use if $dryfrac_{i-1}$ is in the range $[0.15, 0.6]$ and the average temperature on day $i$ is in the range $[0, 40]$ ($C^\circ$).

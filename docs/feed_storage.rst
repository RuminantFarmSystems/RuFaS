Feed Storage Scientific Documentation
======================================

This document details how stored feeds lose dry matter and nutrients.

1 Dry Matter Loss
-----------------

1.1 Grain
~~~~~~~~~

The two types of grain simulated in RuFaS are dry and high-moisture.

The cumulative amount of dry matter lost from dry grain is calculated
with the equation:

.. math::


   L_{gaseous} = M_i \cdot 0.01 \tag{1.1.1}

Where :math:`L_{gaseous}` is the total amount of dry matter lost as gas
(kg) and :math:`M_i` is the mass of dry matter at the time of storage
(kg).

The cumulative amount of dry matter lost from high-moisture grain is
calculated with the equation:

.. math::


   L_{gaseous} = M_i \cdot 0.05 \tag{1.1.2}

Where :math:`{L}_{gaseous}` is the total amount of dry matter lost as
gas (kg) and :math:`M_i` is the mass of dry matter at the time of
storage (kg).

1.2 Hay
~~~~~~~

Four types of hay are simulated by RuFaS: protected-indoor,
protected-wrapped, protected-tarped, and unprotected-outdoor. The
cumulative amount of gaseous dry matter loss that has happened since hay
storage is calculated with the equation:

.. math::


   L_{gaseous} = L_{base} + L_{additional} \tag{1.2.1}

Where :math:`L_{base}` is the total amount of gaseous dry matter loss
from hay after storage (kg) and :math:`L_{additional}` is the total
amount of additional dry matter loss from hay due to weather and storage
conditions (kg).

:math:`L_{base}` is calculated with the equation:

.. math::


   L_{base} = L_{first 30 days} + L_{post 30 days} \tag{1.2.2}

Where :math:`L_{first 30 days}` is the total amount of gaseous dry
matter loss that occurred in the first 30 days of storage (kg), and
:math:`L_{post 30 days}` is the total amount of gaseous dry matter loss
that occurred after the first 30 days of storage (kg).

:math:`L_{first 30 days}` is calculated with the equation:

.. math::


   L_{first 30 days} = \frac{Q + 2433 \cdot [moisture - MF(1 - moisture)]}{(1-moisture)(14206-2433)} \cdot \frac{min(30, d)}{30} \tag{1.2.3}

Where :math:`L_{first 30 days}` is the fraction of gaseous dry matter loss
that occurred in the hay in the first 30 days of storage (:math:`kg`),
:math:`Q` is the total sensible heat generated by the hay
(:math:`kJ/kg`), :math:`moisture` is the fraction of fresh mass in the
hay that is not dry matter when the hay is stored (unitless), :math:`MF`
is the final moisture fraction of hay (unitless), and :math:`d` is the
number of days the hay has been stored for.

The total sensible heat generated is generated with the equation:

.. math::


   Q = 104 \cdot moisture^{2.18} \cdot {b_{density}}^{0.5} + 5.72(moisture^{1.23} \cdot {b_{density}}^{0.94}) \tag{1.2.4}

Where :math:`moisture` is the fraction of fresh mass in the hay that is
not dry matter when the hay is stored (unitless) and :math:`b_{density}`
is the density of the hay bale when stored (:math:`kg / m^3`).

:math:`b_{density}` is calculated with the equation:

.. math::


   b_{density} = 100 + 440 \cdot moisture \tag{1.2.5}

Where :math:`moisture` is the fraction of fresh mass in the hay that is
not dry matter when the hay is stored (unitless).

The final moisture fraction of hay is defined as

.. math::


   MF = 0.12 \tag{1.2.6}

:math:`L_{post 30 days}` is calculated with the equation:

.. math::


   L_{post 30 days} = 0.0001 \cdot max(0, d - 30) \tag{1.2.7}

Where :math:`d` is the number of days that the hay has been stored for.

:math:`L_{additional}` is calculated for protected wrapped, protected
tarped and unprotected outdoor hay with the equation:

.. math::


   L_{additional} = \sum_{i=1}^{d} l \cdot rain_i \cdot max(0.0, \frac{high_i + low_i}{2}) \cdot \frac{1}{b_{density}} \cdot {b_{size}}^3 \tag{1.2.8}

Where :math:`d` is the number of days the hay has been stored for,
:math:`i` is the day since storage that dry matter loss is being
calculated for, :math:`l` is the loss coefficient (unitless),
:math:`rain_i` is the amount of rain on day :math:`i` (cm),
:math:`high_i` is the high temperature on day :math:`i`
(:math:`C^\circ`), :math:`low_i` is the low temperature on day :math:`i`
(:math:`C^\circ`), :math:`b_{density}` is the bale density (:math:`kg / m^3`)
(eqn. 1.2.5), and :math:`b_{size}` is the diameter of the hay bale
(:math:`m`).

The loss coefficients for the different hay storages are defined in the
table: 

===================== =====================
Hay Storage Type      Loss Coefficient
===================== =====================
Protected Wrapped     0.0000216
Protected Tarped      0.0000108
Unprotected Outdoor   0.00006
===================== =====================

Table: :math:`1.2.9`

:math:`L_{additional}` for protected indoor hay is 0.

1.3 Silage and Baleage
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Both silage and baleage experience dry matter loss to fermentation.

The cumulative dry matter loss to fermentation for ensiled alfalfa
(:math:`kg`) is calculated with the equation:

.. math::


   L_{fermentation} = \sum_{i=1}^{d} drymass_{i-1} \cdot (0.0156 - 0.0364(dryfrac_{i-1} - 0.2)) \tag{1.3.1}

Where :math:`d` is the number of days the alfalfa has been ensiled for,
:math:`i` is the day since ensilement that dry matter loss is being
calculated for, :math:`drymass_{i-1}` is the dry matter mass of ensiled
alfalfa on day :math:`i-1`, and :math:`dryfrac_{i-1}` is the fraction of
ensiled alfalfa fresh mass that is dry matter on day :math:`i-1`. When
:math:`i` is 1, :math:`drymass_{i-1}` is the initial amount of ensiled
alfalfa dry mass and :math:`dryfrac_{i-1}` is the initial dry matter
fraction of the ensiled alfalfa. This equation is only appropriate for
use if :math:`dryfrac_{i-1}` is in the range :math:`[0.2, 0.6]` and the
average temperature on day :math:`i` is in the range :math:`[5, 45]`
(:math:`C^\circ`).

The cumulative dry matter loss to fermentation for ensiled corn, grass,
and small grain (:math:`kg`) is calculated with the equation:

.. math::


   L_{fermentation} = \sum_{i=1}^{d} drymass_{i-1} \cdot (0.00864 - 0.0193(dryfrac_{i-1} - 0.15)) \tag{1.3.2}

Where :math:`d` is the number of days the crop has been ensiled for,
:math:`i` is the day since ensilement that dry matter loss is being
calculated for, :math:`drymass_{i-1}` is the dry matter mass of ensiled
crop on day :math:`i-1`, and :math:`dryfrac_{i-1}` is the fraction of
the ensiled crop fresh mass that is dry matter on day :math:`i-1`. When
:math:`i` is 1, :math:`drymass_{i-1}` is the initial amount of ensiled
crop dry mass and :math:`dryfrac_{i-1}` is the initial dry matter
fraction of the ensiled crop. This equation is only appropriate for use
if :math:`dryfrac_{i-1}` is in the range :math:`[0.15, 0.6]` and the
average temperature on day :math:`i` is in the range :math:`[0, 40]`
(:math:`C^\circ`).

1.3.1 Effluent Loss
^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^

Ensiled crops lose both moisture and dry matter mass to effluent (but
baled crops do not).

If a crop is ensiled with a dry matter fraction of less than 0.3, it
will lose dry matter and moisture to effluent.

The cumulative amount of dry matter lost to effluent (:math:`kg`) is
calculated with the equation:

.. math::


   DL_{effluent} = dryfrac_{effluent} \cdot M_{effluent} \cdot 0.1 \cdot max(10, d) \tag{1.3.1.1}

Where :math:`dryfrac_{effluent}` is the fraction of dry matter in the
effluent that is lost, :math:`M_{effluent}` is the estimated maximum
effluent (:math:`kg`), and :math:`d` is the number of days the crop has
been ensiled for.

The cumulative amount of moisture lost to effluent (:math:`kg`) is
calculated with the equation:

.. math::


   ML_{effluent} = (1 - dryfrac_{effluent}) \cdot M_{effluent} \cdot 0.1 \cdot max(10, d) \tag{1.3.1.2}

Where :math:`dryfrac_{effluent}` is the fraction of dry matter in the
effluent that is lost, :math:`M_{effluent}` is the estimated maximum
effluent (:math:`kg`), and :math:`d` is the number of days the crop has
been ensiled for.

The fraction of dry matter in effluent is a constant:

.. math::


   dryfrac_{effluent} = 0.1035 \tag{1.3.1.3}

The estimated maximum effluent for an ensiled crop is calculated with
the following equation:

.. math::


   M_{effluent} = mass_{fresh} \cdot ((1 - dryfrac) - 0.7) \tag{1.3.1.4}

Where :math:`mass_{fresh}` is the fresh mass of the crop when it is
ensiled (:math:`kg`) and :math:`dryfrac` is the fraction of the crop’s
fresh mass which is dry matter when it is ensiled.

2 Nutrient Loss
---------------

Nutrient loss associated with loss of dry matter from stored crops is
calculated for hay, silage and baleage.

The equation for recalculating the percentage of dry matter nurient:

.. math::


   n_{updated} = \frac{max(0, \frac{n_{initial}}{100} - C) \cdot DL_{fraction}}{1 - DL_{fraction}} \cdot 100 \tag{2.1}

Where :math:`n_{updated}` is the percentage of the target nutrient in
the stored crop’s dry matter mass after accounting for dry matter loss,
:math:`n_{initial}` is the percentage of the target nutrient in the
stored crop’s dry matter mass before accounting for dry matter loss,
:math:`C` is the fractional loss coefficient specific to the nutrient
and storage type, and :math:`DL_{fraction}` is the fraction of dry
matter lost.

:math:`DL_{fraction}` is calculated with the equation:

.. math::


   DL_{fraction} = \frac{DM_{updated}}{DM_{initial}} \tag{2.2}

Where :math:`DM_{updated}` is the dry matter mass in the stored crop
after accounting for dry matter loss (:math:`kg`), and
:math:`DM_{initial}` is the amount of dry matter mass in the stored crop
before accounting for dry matter loss (:math:`kg`).

2.2.1 Nutrient Loss to Effluent
^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^

When dry matter is lost to effluent from an ensiled crop, the percentages
of crude protein and non-protein nitrogen must be recalculated for that crop.

The percentage of crude protein (CP) is recalculated with the equation:

.. math::


   CP_{updated} = \frac{CP_{initial} \cdot 0.01 - 0.3 \cdot DL_{fraction}}{1 - DL_{fraction}} \cdot 100 \tag{2.2.1.1}

Where :math:`CP_{initial}` is the percentage of dry matter mass that is
crude protein in the ensiled crop before accounting for dry matter loss
to effluent and :math:`DL_{fraction}` is the fraction of dry matter mass
lost to effluent. The percentage of crude protein is lower-bounded at 0.

The percentage of non-protein nitrogen (NPN) is calculated with the equation:

.. math::

   NPN_{updated} = \frac{(NPN_{initial} \cdot 0.01) \cdot (CP_{initial} \cdot 0.01) - 0.3 \cdot DL_{fraction}}{(CP_{initial} \cdot 0.01) - DL_{fraction}} \cdot 100 \tag{2.2.1.2}

Where :math:`NPN_{initial}` is percentage of dry matter mass that is
non-protein nitrogen in the ensiled crop before accouting for dry matter
loss to effluent, :math:`CP_{initial}` is the percentage of dry matter
mass that is crude protein before accounting for dry matter loss to
effluent, and :math:`DL_{fraction}` is the fraction of dry matter mass
lost to effluent. The percentage of non-protein nitrogen is lower-bounded
at 0.

:math:`DL_{fraction}` is calculated with the equation:

.. math::


   DL_{fraction} = \frac{DM_{lost}}{DM_{initial}} \tag{2.2.1.3}

Where :math:`DM_{lost}` is the amount of dry matter mass in the
ensiled crop after accounting for dry matter lost to effluent, and
:math:`DM_{initial}` is the amount of dry matter mass in the ensiled
crop before accounting for dry matter lost to effluent.
